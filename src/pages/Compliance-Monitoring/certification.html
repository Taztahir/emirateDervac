Compliance Monitoring – Certification

The Compliance Monitoring Engineering - Certification (CME-C) section is responsible for the establishment of Emirates Engineering standards for qualifying and evaluating certifying staff and issuance of privileges and limitations based on operator’s national aviation regulations and the level of trainings and extent of experience of individual certifying staff.

With the established baseline qualification criteria, CME-C evaluate each candidate certifying staff through series of test and board interviews; and validation of their training and extent of practical experiences for adequacy and currency to ensure only those with right behaviours, attitude, skills and technical competencies will be granted with certification authorisations.

Certification Authorisation issued by the CME-C section permit the holder to exercise certain privileges on behalf of the organisation that includes:

  • Certification of Aircraft Systems and Components during Line Maintenance
  • Certification of Engine/APU Systems and Component On/Off aircraft
  • Certification of Electrical, Avionics and LRU Equipment On/Off Aircraft
  • Certification of Aircraft Release to Service after Line or Base Maintenance
  • Certification of Components Release to Service after Workshop Maintenance
  • Certification of Component Restoration, Testing, Inspection and/or Calibration
  • Certification of Airworthiness Review for Aircraft Continuing Airworthiness

CME-Certification section responsibilities also encompass the establishment of qualification criteria for the approval and issuance of authorisations to Emirates Engineering Training Centre qualified personnel to conduct the GCAA/EASA-approved aircraft type courses, engine and/or component specific training, theoretical examinations, on-the-job practical assessments and other aviation-related training activities. The CME-Certification section centrally processed, managed and control application, evaluation, issuance, renewal, withdrawal and retention of consequent records of certifying staff authorisation through its secured electronic authorisation database called “Tarkhis”. All Authorisation approval are issued electronically while the list of all approval holders are regularly published in the Emirates Engineering portal.
